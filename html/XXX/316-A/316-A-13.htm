TITLE XXX
OCCUPATIONS AND PROFESSIONS

CHAPTER 316-A
CHIROPRACTIC

Section 316-A:13

    316-A:13 Examinations. –
    I. The board shall give a written examination in the following subjects: Anatomy, physiology, symptomatology, hygiene, chiropractic orthopedy, histology, pathology, chiropractic x-ray procedures and analysis, neurology, bacteriology, and chemistry, and the principles of chiropractic adjusting and nerve tracing. The examination shall address the basic professional standards of chiropractic relating only to the determination of and adjustment of vertebral misalignments (subluxations) of the spine and specifically excluding treatments using ancillary techniques and modalities including, but not limited to, heat, water, and the like.
    II. This section shall not be superseded by administrative rulemaking.

Source. 1988, 87:2, eff. April 18, 1988.

TITLE XXXI
TRADE AND COMMERCE

CHAPTER 350-B
UNIFORM TRADE SECRETS ACT

Section 350-B:5

    350-B:5 Preservation of Secrecy. – In an action under this chapter, a court shall preserve the secrecy of an alleged trade secret by reasonable means, which may include granting protective orders in connection with discovery proceedings, holding in-camera hearings, sealing the records of the action, and ordering any person involved in the litigation not to disclose an alleged trade secret without prior court approval.

Source. 1989, 220:1, eff. Jan. 1, 1990.
